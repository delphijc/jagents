# SKILL: Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)

**Purpose:** Enterprise GRC program management and implementation  
**Domain:** Compliance, risk

 management, policy governance, audit coordination  
**Type:** Comprehensive Security Management Skill

---

## Overview

The GRC skill encompasses the complete lifecycle of Governance, Risk, and Compliance management for enterprise security programs. It integrates policy development, risk assessment, compliance validation, and audit coordination to ensure organizational security objectives align with regulatory requirements and business goals.

- Safeguards and sub-controls

- Monitor and test networks
- Maintain information security policy

- Technical safeguards
- Privacy Rule compliance
- Breach notification requirements

#### ISO 27001 (Information Security)

## üìã Key Activities

### Policy Governance

#### Development
1. Identify policy needs
2. Research requirements and best practices
3. Draft policy content
4. Stakeholder review and approval
5. Publication and communication
6. Training and awareness

#### Maintenance
1. Periodic review (annual/biennial)
2. Update based on changes
3. Version control
4. Retirement of obsolete policies
5. Archive management

### Risk Management Process

#### Assessment Cycle
1. **Asset Inventory**
   - Identify critical assets
   - Classify by sensitivity
   - Map dependencies
   
2. **Threat Analysis**
   - Identify threat sources
   - Analyze threat capabilities
   - Assess likelihood
   
3. **Vulnerability Assessment**
   - Technical vulnerabilities (CVE, CWE)
   - Process vulnerabilities
   - Human factors
   
4. **Risk Calculation**
   - Impact determination
   - Likelihood assessment
   - Risk level assignment
   - Prioritization
   
5. **Risk Treatment**
   - Accept
   - Mitigate
   - Transfer
   - Avoid

#### Continuous Monitoring
- Automated control testing
- Manual control reviews
- Key metric tracking
- Dashboard reporting
- Exception management

### Compliance Management

#### Compliance Lifecycle
1. **Requirements Analysis**
   - Identify applicable regulations
   - Map requirements to controls
   - Document obligations
   
2. **Gap Assessment**
   - Compare current state to requirements
   - Identify gaps
   - Prioritize remediation
   
3. **Implementation**
   - Deploy controls
   - Configure systems
   - Implement processes
   - Train personnel
   
4. **Validation**
   - Control testing
   - Evidence collection
   - Documentation review
   - Compliance verification
   
5. **Reporting**
   - Compliance status dashboards
   - Executive reporting
   - Audit readiness assessments
   - Regulatory submissions

#### Audit Management
1. **Pre-Audit Preparation**
   - Review requirements
   - Gather evidence
   - Conduct internal assessment
   - Address known gaps
   
2. **Audit Execution**
   - Coordinate with auditors
   - Provide evidence
   - Respond to questions
   - Document findings
   
3. **Remediation**
   - Analyze findings
   - Develop corrective actions
   - Implement fixes
   - Validate remediation
   
4. **Post-Audit**
   - Lessons learned
   - Process improvements
   - Update documentation
   - Prepare for next audit
